My experience in ENG 2003 exceeded expectations by connecting communication directly to engineering practice. Communication Development

Before this course, my technical communication was competent but limited in scope. I could explain concepts to fellow students but struggled with non-technical audiences. Now I approach communication as a core engineering competency deserving systematic attention.

My greatest improvement was in presentation skills through the assertion-evidence method. My technical writing also became more purposeful and audience-focused. However, I still need to work on adapting communication styles for different cultural contexts and improving collaborative discussion facilitation.

This course will significantly impact my remaining studies and future practice. I now view communication as integral to technical work, not separate from it. These skills will be essential for team collaboration, stakeholder communication, and career advancement in engineering.
