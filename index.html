Professional Reflection

Course Experience (ENG 2003)

My ENG 2003 experience was more than I was expecting as it tied communication to engineering practice. The logical method to writing technical phrases and presentation of statements with evidence has already enhanced my works in other units. The 7 Cs framework and audience analysis altered my basic assumptions and approaches to any communication-related project.

Learning how to organize technical reports and generate interesting presentations proved to be the most important material. The module of engineering argumentation and especially the aspects concerning ethos, pathos, and logos combination allowed me to understand why some technical proposals can be successfully adopted and other fail.

Communication Development

Prior to this course, I was skilled and knowledgeable with respect to technical communication, although I had only a limited scope. I was able to explain technical ideas to other students but I found it harder to explain ideas to non-technical audiences. Now I am thinking of communication as a fundamental skill in engineering that can and should be addressed systematically.

The most effective enhancement process that I underwent was on presentation skills using the assertion-evidence approach. My technical writing is also now more the purposeful and audience-centered. Nonetheless, I am yet to work on communicating in a culturally-specific context and the facilitation of discussions.

The course will immensely influence the course of my further studies and practice. Communication is no longer seen by me as a language which is apart when doing technical work. Such competencies will be vital toward teamwork, communicating with stakeholders, and enhancing their careers in engineering.
